Season 1 Synopsis

Part A: Dysfunction of the Status Quo

The season opens in total chaos. The previous crew was murdered (without anyone even bothering to investigate why). The morning after the massacre, the main computer activates the "Judith Protocol" and sends a team of seven strangers who have nothing in common but shared Jewish DNA and varying traumas. They do not get along, not because they are monsters, but because they are too human: the Captain is a leadership fantasist living in a war movie, Jordan is a progressive who thinks every situation is a workshop in sensitivity, Schtempepper is a nihilist who would rather the ship explode than admit he was wrong, and Rabbi Itzik and Dr. Khalil are simply hustlers of survival. and Lilly...oh Lilly. Inside this tin can, every mission to integrate into a new planet turns into a family fight at a Friday night dinner, only the fate of the galaxy is at stake. They will not be able to fit in anywhere, because they cannot even sit in one room without wanting to kill each other.

Part B: The Transformation (or: How to Learn to Love the Monster Next to You)

When they realize that no one in the universe is even willing to sell them coffee, something changes. Under the infinite pressure, their egos begin to crack. The Captain starts to understand that the rules are just a suggestion. Schtempepper finds some comfort in the fact that someone actually appreciates his cursed genius, and Lilly, the alien who came to destroy them, starts asking herself why the hell she enjoys this human neurosis. They turn from a crew of strangers into a toxic, yet effective, family unit.

Part C: Consequences and Collapse

This is where everything explodes. It turns out that every planet they visited was rendered uninhabitable because of their stupidity. It is not just an antisemitic universe. it is a universe united in its disgust toward the "Judith" expedition. The Anti-Jewish Federation rises, and they are coming to end what remains. Just before they are wiped off the map, they discover the season's shocking revelation: there is a parallel universe. A universe where Jews are not only loved, they are the stars of the place. Will they run away there for safe haven? Or will they decide to fight for the jews in this jew-hating universe, even if it is about to explode?

Our Vision

Our vision is simple: to create the most unhinged, unapologetic, and boundary-shattering animated series the screen has ever seen. Operating in the wild-west of web animation means no suits, no censorship, and zero permissions asked. We are here to weaponize taboos, stir up chaos, and make some serious, global noise.

But don't let the madness fool you. Beneath the sci-fi chaos lies a razor-sharp mission: to wage war on modern antisemitism. We aren't here to lecture. Instead, we take historical trauma and current events and flip them into brilliant galactic metaphors that hit you right in basic human logic. We are here to systematically dismantle the cynical excuse that "anti-Zionism is not antisemitism."

We have absolutely no intention of sugarcoating reality or painting Jews as flawless, holy "beacon of light." Our characters are deeply flawed, chaotic, and messy. Just like every other human being in the universe. And that's the whole point. Through the bizarre alien civilizations we build, we hold a mirror up to the world's blatant double standards and put the hypocrisy of antisemitism on blast.

This series is our ultimate statement: We are officially done hiding. We are done apologizing for our identity. Deal with it.
